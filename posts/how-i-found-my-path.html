How I Found My Path to Frontend Development

Growing up, I was never the kid who loved school or reading books. To be honest, I often found it boring and struggled to stay motivated. As I got older, I spent several years working at a fast food chain. It was steady work, but I knew I wanted something different — something that would challenge me and offer a real career path.

That desire led me to join the Army. I hoped the discipline and structure would give me direction, but after some time, I realized it just wasn’t the right fit for me. Looking back, the experience taught me a lot about myself, and it pushed me to find a career I could genuinely be passionate about.

Back in high school, I had an interest in computers, even though I wasn’t a strong student. I took classes like A+ and networking, and even attempted the A+ certification exam — but I didn’t pass. Still, that spark for IT never really went away.

After leaving the Army, I enrolled in a vocational school to study Information Technology. Unfortunately, I made some poor decisions during that time, and before I knew it, I found myself back in the fast food industry. It felt like I was stuck in a loop — but I wasn’t ready to give up.

Eventually, I moved to a new place where I could get support and focus on building a better future. That’s when I discovered web development. I had actually heard about it before — one of my coworkers in the Army was learning it too. At the time, I didn’t pursue it because I thought you had to be a genius, like engineers or doctors, to succeed. But out of curiosity, I started researching what it really takes to become a web developer.

I quickly learned that HTML, CSS, and JavaScript form the foundation of web development. Excited, I dove in and started learning them. But I ran into challenges, especially with JavaScript. There were times when it felt like I wasn’t learning fast enough or understanding things the way I should.

To switch things up, I explored backend development and started learning Python — mostly out of interest, not with a clear career goal. Python felt easier to grasp, except for concepts like Object-Oriented Programming, which still confuse me. When I looked at the full roadmap for backend development, it felt overwhelming how much there was to learn.

Even so, I kept going, but dealing with constant bugs and errors was mentally draining. I often had to step away, clear my head, and come back with a fresh perspective.

Through all of this, I realized my real interest is in frontend development — building the parts of websites and apps that people actually interact with. It combines creativity and logic, and that’s exactly what I enjoy. My path hasn’t been straightforward or easy, but I’m proud of how far I’ve come and excited about what’s ahead.
